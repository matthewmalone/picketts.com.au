Who Are We?

We are a group of residents living in the Kincumber and Picketts Valley area who are concerned about the proposed development of 210 manufactured houses in a residential land lease community and includes associated supporting service and related infrastructure and community and social facilities for the use of residents that is proposed to be built by Living Choice Australia Pty Ltd on the land formerly known as Bangaloe Stud located at 255, 255A, 255B Avoca Drive & 19 Picketts Valley Road, Kincumber.

We have formed the Kincumber and Picketts Valley Community Action Group (KPVCAG) in order to focus our concerns and share information to keep residents and the wider Kincumber, Picketts Valley, Avoca Beach and Terrigal communities aware of the impact of the proposed development.

We are very concerned about overdevelopment in the area, the urbanisation of rural land, a further strain on the traffic using Avoca Drive, and the impact of such a large development on the environment, wildlife and the Avoca Lagoon Catchment area. Those properties that share common boundaries with the proposed site will have to contend with traffic and noise as 400-500 cars from new houses, visitors, employees who exit and enter the site.

The wider community using Avoca Drive, Scenic Highway, Picketts Valley Road, and Melville street will find further delays on already struggling roads. We are concerned about one possible proposed construction zone being in Picketts Valley Road and a final entry and exit to the new estate being in Avoca Drive opposite Melville Street. The two sites are already traffic black-spots. We strongly believe that the density of 210 manufactured houses and infrastructure on the proposed site does not alignment with the "low-impact rural housing" that is specified in the E4 Zoning in the Gosford Local Environment Plan 2014.

What Do We Know So Far?

Only some residents in the Kincumber area who have adjoining properties to the proposed 'manufactured housing estate' have been notified so far via a letter in mailboxes. This letter dated 27 September 2019 from Mark Sargent, AIGIS Group, Mark Sargent Enterprises advises of a Development Application:Residential Land Lease Community (Over-50's Lifestyle Development) which "is being prepared for lodgement with Central Coast Council (CCC) by the proponent, Living Choice Australia Pty Ltd, in respect of the proposed project." Further, Mr Sargent writes that "this firm has been engaged to prepare the SIA and EIA, and conduct associated consultation/engagement to inform those reports."

The letter states that the development will be compliant with the current zoning (E4 Environmental Living) under the Gosford Local Environment Plan 2014, which permits development as a caravan park.
