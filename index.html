Who are we?

We are a group of residents living in the Kincumber and Picketts Valley area who are deeply concerned about the proposed development of 202 manufactured houses in a' residential land lease community' proposed to be built by Living Choice Australia Pty Ltd on the land formerly known as Bangaloe Stud located at 255, 255A, 255B Avoca Drive & 19 Picketts Valley Road, Kincumber. Living Choice's proposal also includes the building of associated supporting service and related infrastructure and community and social facilities for the use of their residents on this site.

We have formed the Kincumber and Picketts Valley Community Action Group (KPVCAG) in order to focus our concerns and share information to keep residents and the wider Kincumber, Picketts Valley, Avoca Beach and Terrigal communities aware of the impact of the proposed development.

We are very concerned about overdevelopment in the area, the urbanisation of rural land, a further strain on the traffic using Avoca Drive, and the impact of such a large development on the environment, wildlife and the Avoca Lagoon Catchment area. Those properties that share common boundaries with the proposed site will have to contend with traffic and noise as 400-500 cars from new houses, visitors, employees who exit and enter the site.

The wider community using Avoca Drive, Scenic Highway, Picketts Valley Road, and Melville street will find further delays on already struggling roads. We are concerned about one possible proposed construction zone being in Picketts Valley Road and a final entry and exit to the new estate being in Avoca Drive opposite Melville Street. The two sites are already traffic black-spots. We strongly believe that the density of 210 manufactured houses and infrastructure on the proposed site it not aligned with the "low-impact rural housing" that is specified in the E4 Zoning in the Gosford Local Environment Plan 2014.
